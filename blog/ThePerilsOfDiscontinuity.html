Challenges of Organizational Dynamics

People who came together to help an organisation succeed actually end up delighting in each other’s failures and resenting each other’s success. We withhold information and resources from one another, try to control one another, and blame one another. When I am blaming A or B or department X, Y, or Z and suggesting that all our problems could be solved if only they would straighten up, I am doing it because their shortcomings justify my failure to improve.

It begins as a conflict between two persons and spreads to a war between many; finally, organisations are filled with people whose energies are largely spent on sustaining illusion.

The State of Education in India

Has India’s education system become a contradiction in terms, in that it does not educate and is no longer a system—an orderly arrangement of ideas and functions formulated to achieve a desired objective? A spate of scams, of mass-copying, leaked question papers, and faked certificates, poses a threat to the groves of academe which are being turned into a blackboard jungle, producing hordes of purportedly educated unemployed young people, making a cruel mockery of our vaunted ‘demographic dividend’.

Tourism and Environmental Challenges

Tourism bucks and real estate greed are still seen running rampant. Whether travellers come looking for physical or spiritual adventures, they become part of the same traffic jams and garbage mountains. Tiger reserves to once-pristine beaches, too often the scene is sadly the same. They crowd onto railway tracks to see Dudhsagar Falls, make Darjeeling run short of drinking water, make Ooty feel indistinguishable from Bengaluru. This is a painful irony. The search for an escape from cities turns tourist destinations into an unpleasant reflection of these cities.
